About the hospital

The college initially functioned at General Hospital, Ernakulam, and Indira Gandhi Cooperative Hospital, Kochi and classes were conducted at Jawaharlal Nehru International Stadium complex, Kochi. Land was acquired at HMT Estate at Kalamassery and the foundation stone for the campus was laid by Shri E.K. Nayanar, the then Chief Minister of Kerala State. The college was shifted to the present campus at Kalamassery in 2004 and was inaugurated by Shri A.K. Antony, Chief Minister of Kerala. Dr P.G.R. Pillai was the founding Principal of the college.

Sophisticated facilities of world class standards which provides treatment for all most all medical conditions with renowned Doctors specialized in all major Disciplines and low cost packages for all treatments makes the college a unique from others.... The College meets all the requirements of the MCI for training medical interns and has got complete access to all the clinical and super specialty departments of the Medical College Hospital.

The hospital is equipped with 700 beds. Of these, 500 are in the general category and meet the requirements stipulated by the Medical Council of India (MCI) for the teaching departments. The remaining beds belong to the private, semiprivate and the unitary wards of the specialty and super-specialty departments.

Presenty a new superspeciality block which will contain the Mother and Child wing is under construction. The work is scheduled to be completed by the fag end of 2020
